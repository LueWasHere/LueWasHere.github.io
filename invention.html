Invention.

ASCII art, also known as character art, is a type of art that was created using the characters from the ASCII (American Standard Code for Information Interchange) character set. ASCII art gained popularity in the 1960s and 1970s, as computers were becoming more widely used, and has continued to evolve and gain popularity to this day.
The ASCII character set includes 128 characters, including letters, numbers, symbols, and control characters. ASCII art was created by arranging these characters in a way that would create an image or design. The art was typically created using a simple text editor, such as Notepad, and saved as a text file.
One of the earliest examples of ASCII art was a picture of a heart created by a programmer named Robert W. Bemer in 1965. This simple image was created using only three characters: "<", "3", and ">".
As computer graphics technology advanced, ASCII art became more sophisticated. Artists began to use more complex character sets, such as ANSI (American National Standards Institute), and developed techniques to create shading and depth in their images. Some artists even began to use color in their ASCII art by using characters with different backgrounds or foreground colors.
Today, ASCII art continues to be a popular form of digital art, with dedicated communities of artists and enthusiasts. It has also found its way into popular culture, with ASCII art appearing in movies, video games, and even on clothing.
ASCII art was created by using the characters from the ASCII character set to create images and designs. It has a rich history and has evolved alongside advances in computer technology. Despite its simplicity, ASCII art remains a unique and popular form of digital art today.

Follow up.

ASCII art has a long and interesting history, and has been used in a variety of different contexts over the years. One of the early applications of ASCII art was in early computer games and demos. Because early computers didn't have the processing power to display complex graphics, programmers would often use ASCII art to create simple graphics and animations. This led to the development of the "ANSI art" style, which used a larger character set to create more detailed and colorful images.
ASCII art was also popular in the early days of the internet, particularly in online forums and chat rooms. Because ASCII art could be easily created and shared using simple text editors, it became a popular way for users to express themselves and add visual flair to their messages.
In recent years, ASCII art has seen a resurgence in popularity thanks to social media and the rise of "memes". Social media platforms like Twitter and Instagram have made it easy to share ASCII art with a wider audience, and many artists have gained a following by sharing their work online.
One interesting development in the world of ASCII art is the use of algorithms and machine learning to create more sophisticated images. Researchers have developed algorithms that can analyze photographs and convert them into ASCII art, allowing for more complex and detailed images than would be possible with manual techniques.
Despite its simplicity, ASCII art remains a unique and powerful form of digital art. Its ability to convey emotion and create atmosphere using nothing but simple characters is a testament to the power of creativity and imagination. Whether you're a seasoned artist or a curious beginner, ASCII art is definitely worth exploring.
